Our story

Naan was always on the side. We make it the star.

A concept by Chef Vineet Bhatia MBE and his son Ronit Bhatia, built with Emirates Flight Catering, at Emirates Group Headquarters, Dubai.

The Concept

Naan & Co runs on one conviction: India’s most iconic bread has spent long enough in the background. We moved it forward, pressed into naanwiches, stretched into pizzaans, fired fresh in a tandoor in full view, every time.

This isn’t reinvention for its own sake. It’s overdue recognition. The same bread that’s fed generations, now built for how people eat today: fast, direct, without cutting a single corner on flavour or technique.

What that looks like in practice

Naanwiches take the format everyone already knows, pressed, loaded, grilled, and rebuild it from the ground up around naan instead of bread. Pizzaans go further still: naan as the base, not the side, fired until it blisters, carrying flavour combinations that owe nothing to convention.

Food as theatre, not just fuel

Every naan at Naan & Co is fired in front of the guest, not behind a wall. The slap of dough against a hot tandoor wall, the char forming in real time, the moment it lands on the tray. We didn’t want to hide the craft. We wanted to make it the show.

Built for a generation that wants both

Today’s diner doesn’t choose between heritage and speed, between authenticity and convenience. Naan & Co exists precisely at that intersection: real technique, real flavour, delivered at the pace of a busy day.

That’s the vision, in full. Not a trend, a correction. The long-overdue moment naan stopped waiting for its turn.

The Heritage & Vision

MBE investiture, Buckingham Palace

The first Indian chef in history to earn a Michelin star. Everything Naan & Co does starts here.

In 2001, Chef Vineet Bhatia MBE made culinary history: awarded a Michelin star at his London restaurant, Zaika, becoming the first chef of Indian heritage ever to receive one. It wasn’t a lucky night in one kitchen. It was the moment the world was forced to reconsider what Indian cuisine could be.

That moment launched a movement. Vineet went on to pioneer Indian Progressive Cuisine, a style built on the belief that classical Indian flavour deserved the same precision, restraint and artistry as the finest European kitchens, not a lesser version of it. It’s a philosophy that has since defined his restaurants under some of hospitality’s most respected names, including Harrods, Marriott and The Oberoi Group, carried to dining rooms around the world.

His influence reaches well beyond the pass. Appointed a culinary ambassador for the UK’s GREAT Campaign, he has carried British gastronomic excellence to a global stage. He has authored two cookbooks, mentored a generation of chefs, and lent his expertise as a judge on Netflix’s The Final Table and MasterChef India. In recognition of a career spent redefining what Indian cuisine can be, he holds an MBE, a rare honour, and a fitting one.

Naan & Co is where that legacy finds its most direct expression yet. Working alongside his son, Ronit Bhatia, shaped by a career in fast-moving startups and scale-ups, Vineet’s technique is translated into a format built for how people actually eat today. Ronit leads the day-to-day craft: the concept, the menu, the design, the attitude. Together, father and son have built something neither could have alone: Michelin-pedigree precision, delivered at the pace of modern life.

The Powerhouse Alliance

Naan & Co is powered by one of the most formidable culinary operations on earth.

Behind every great concept is the infrastructure that lets it scale without ever diluting its standards. For Naan & Co, that infrastructure is Emirates Flight Catering (EKFC), a name that commands respect across the aviation and hospitality world for one simple reason: nobody does large-scale culinary excellence at this level.

EKFC isn’t just a caterer. It’s one of the largest and most technically sophisticated food operations on the planet, engineered to deliver flawless consistency, at extraordinary volume, to some of the most demanding standards in the industry, day after day, without exception. This is precision gastronomy operating at a scale most kitchens could never imagine, and doing it flawlessly.

What sets EKFC apart isn’t just its size. It’s a relentless drive to push further: constantly refining process, technology and technique in pursuit of a standard that never plateaus. Where most large-scale operations trade quality for volume, EKFC has spent years proving that trade-off doesn’t have to exist. It is, without exaggeration, a leading force in what luxury gastronomy looks like when it’s built to operate at global scale.

That relentlessness is exactly what makes this alliance so significant. Chef Vineet Bhatia MBE brings the culinary authorship: the Michelin-pedigree vision of what naan can become. EKFC brings the operational firepower to execute that vision without compromise, at a level of consistency few standalone restaurants could ever achieve alone.

This is not a chef simply using a caterer. It’s a genuine alliance between two powerhouses, one built on culinary mastery, the other on operational supremacy, united by the same uncompromising instinct: never settle for good enough. It’s the reason Naan & Co isn’t just a concept with potential. It’s a concept with the machinery behind it to go as far as its ambition can take it.
